Deep Learning Showcase

Apr 18, 2023 2 min read

Due to the considerable size of the notebooks associated with this post, hosting and running them locally is not feasible. Instead, I will provide links to each of the notebooks, offering an in-depth look into the techniques utilized in this deep learning project.

News Article Section Prediction

In part 1 of the project, we aim to predict news article section headings utilizing the Guardian News dataset. This involves comparing RNN variants, delving into embeddings with CNNs, contrasting with non-neural approaches, incorporating additional data, and preserving the optimal models. Here’s the notebook for a deeper exploration.

Transfer Learning

The focus of this segment is on employing transfer learning and constructing models from the ground up to forecast the topic of news articles using the text from the BBC Articles Full Text and Category Dataset.

This notebook will import the best models from the previous section and utilize transfer learning methods to tailor them to this new task, in addition to developing fresh models for a comparative analysis. Here’s the notebook for further details.

Generative Models

The objective of this section is to develop a generative model capable of producing sentences for news articles corresponding to the two most prevalent genre/section types identified in the dataset. This involves leveraging LSTM-based architectures alongside other pertinent techniques. Here’s the notebook for in-depth exploration.

Reflecting on Improvements

The objective of this project was to apply deep learning techniques within a practical context. In hindsight, dedicating additional time to dataset preparation would have resulted in better results.
